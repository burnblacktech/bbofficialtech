# ITR-4 (Sugam) — Step-by-Step Filing Guide

> For individuals/HUFs opting for presumptive taxation (no books needed)

## Who Should File ITR-4?
- Small business owners (turnover ≤ ₹2 crore under Section 44AD)
- Professionals (receipts ≤ ₹75 lakh under Section 44ADA)
- Goods transport operators (Section 44AE)
- **Total income ≤ ₹50 lakh**
- **No business losses to carry forward**
- **No capital gains or foreign income** (use ITR-2/3 for those)

## Presumptive Taxation — What Is It?
Instead of maintaining books of accounts and claiming actual expenses, you declare a **minimum percentage** of your turnover as income:

| Section | Who | Minimum Income |
|---------|-----|----------------|
| 44AD | Business | 8% of cash turnover, 6% of digital turnover |
| 44ADA | Professionals | 50% of gross receipts |
| 44AE | Goods transport | ₹7,500 per vehicle per month |

**Benefit**: No books of accounts, no audit, simple filing.

## Step-by-Step Flow on BurnBlack

### Step 1–2: Same as ITR-1
Start filing, fill personal information.

### Step 3: Presumptive Business Income
1. Add **"Business"** source in the left panel
2. System detects presumptive entries → auto-selects ITR-4
3. Click **"Add Entry"**
4. Fill:
   - **Section**: 44AD (business) or 44ADA (professional) or 44AE (transport)
   - **Business name** and nature
   - **Gross receipts/turnover**
   - **Digital receipts > 95%?** (checkbox) → enables ₹3Cr threshold and 6% rate
   - **Declared income** (must be ≥ minimum: 8%/6%/50% of receipts)
5. System enforces:
   - If declared income < minimum → auto-uses minimum
   - If turnover > ₹2Cr (44AD) → error: "Use ITR-3"
   - If receipts > ₹75L (44ADA) → error: "Use ITR-3"

### Step 4: Salary Income (if applicable)
Same as ITR-1. Many small business owners also have salary income.

### Step 5: Other Income
Same as ITR-1 (interest, dividends, etc.)

### Step 6: House Property (if applicable)
Same as ITR-1 (one property only for ITR-4).

### Step 7: Deductions
Same as ITR-1 (80C, 80D, etc. for old regime).

### Step 8: Bank Details & Submit
Same as ITR-1.

## Examples

### Example 1: Shopkeeper (44AD, cash business)
- Annual turnover: ₹80 lakh (all cash)
- Minimum deemed income: ₹80L × 8% = **₹6.4 lakh**
- Can declare higher if actual profit is more
- No books needed, no audit

### Example 2: Freelance Developer (44AD, digital)
- Annual receipts: ₹40 lakh (95%+ via bank transfer)
- Minimum deemed income: ₹40L × 6% = **₹2.4 lakh**
- Digital threshold: ₹3 crore (eligible for ITR-4)
- No books needed, no audit

### Example 3: Doctor (44ADA)
- Annual receipts: ₹60 lakh
- Minimum deemed income: ₹60L × 50% = **₹30 lakh**
- Can declare higher if actual profit is more
- No books needed, no audit
- If receipts > ₹75L → must switch to ITR-3

### Example 4: Truck Owner (44AE)
- 3 trucks, operated all 12 months
- Deemed income: 3 × ₹7,500 × 12 = **₹2.7 lakh**
- No books needed

## Key Limits & Thresholds

| Rule | Limit | Consequence of Exceeding |
|------|-------|--------------------------|
| 44AD turnover (cash) | ₹2 crore | Must file ITR-3 |
| 44AD turnover (95%+ digital) | ₹3 crore | Must file ITR-3 |
| 44ADA receipts | ₹75 lakh | Must file ITR-3 |
| Total income | ₹50 lakh | Must file ITR-2 or ITR-3 |
| Opting out of 44AD | — | Must maintain books for next 5 years |

## Tax Computation
Same slab rates as ITR-1. Presumptive income is added to gross total income.

### New Regime (AY 2026-27)
- Deemed income ₹6.4L → Standard deduction ₹75K → Taxable ₹5.65L
- Tax: 0-4L @ 0% + 4-5.65L @ 5% = ₹8,250
- Rebate: ₹5.65L ≤ ₹12L → full rebate → **Zero tax**

### Old Regime
- Deemed income ₹6.4L → Deductions (80C ₹1.5L + 80D ₹25K) → Taxable ₹4.65L
- Tax: 0-2.5L @ 0% + 2.5-4.65L @ 5% = ₹10,750
- Rebate: ₹4.65L ≤ ₹5L → full rebate → **Zero tax**

## Advance Tax
- If presumptive income → entire advance tax due by **March 15**
- No quarterly installments required (unlike regular business)
- Penalty for non-payment: interest u/s 234C
