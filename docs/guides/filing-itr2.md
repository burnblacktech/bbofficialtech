# ITR-2 — Step-by-Step Filing Guide

> For individuals with capital gains, foreign income, or multiple properties

## Who Should File ITR-2?
- Capital gains (sold shares, mutual funds, property, crypto)
- Foreign income or foreign assets
- More than one house property
- Director/partner in a company
- Income > ₹50 lakh (disqualified from ITR-1)
- Agricultural income > ₹5,000
- **No business/professional income** (use ITR-3/4 for that)

## Step-by-Step Flow on BurnBlack

### Step 1–2: Same as ITR-1
Start filing, fill personal information (PAN, Aadhaar, address).

### Step 3: Salary Income (if applicable)
Same as ITR-1. Upload Form 16 or enter manually.

### Step 4: Capital Gains
1. Add **"Capital Gains"** source in the left panel
2. Click **"Add Transaction"**
3. For each sale, fill:
   - **Asset type**: Listed equity, Mutual fund, Property, Debt MF, Gold, Crypto/VDA, Other
   - **Section**: 111A (STCG equity), 112A (LTCG equity), 112 (LTCG other)
   - **Purchase date** and **Sale date** (determines STCG vs LTCG)
   - **Purchase value** and **Sale value**
   - **Expenses** (brokerage, stamp duty)
4. System auto-classifies:
   - Held < 12 months (equity) or < 24 months (property) → STCG
   - Held longer → LTCG
5. **Shortcut**: Upload broker CG statement for auto-fill

### Capital Gains Tax Rates (AY 2025-26+)
| Type | Rate | Exemption |
|------|------|-----------|
| STCG equity (s.111A) | 20% | None |
| LTCG equity (s.112A) | 12.5% | First ₹1.25L exempt |
| LTCG property/other (s.112) | 12.5% | No indexation |
| STCG other | Slab rates | None |
| Crypto/VDA | 30% flat | No deductions allowed |

### Step 5: Foreign Income (if applicable)
1. Add **"Foreign Income"** source
2. For each foreign income:
   - Country, income type, amount (in INR)
   - Tax paid abroad (for DTAA credit)
3. System computes Foreign Tax Credit:
   - Credit = min(tax paid abroad, proportionate Indian tax)

### Step 6: House Property
Same as ITR-1, but ITR-2 allows **multiple properties**.
- First self-occupied: interest deduction up to ₹2L
- Additional properties: deemed let-out (taxed on notional rent)

### Step 7: Other Income
Same as ITR-1.

### Step 8: Deductions
Same as ITR-1 (80C, 80D, etc. for old regime).

### Step 9: Bank Details & Submit
Same as ITR-1.

### Step 10: Post-Filing
Same as ITR-1. E-verify within 30 days.

## Key Differences from ITR-1
| Feature | ITR-1 | ITR-2 |
|---------|-------|-------|
| Income limit | ₹50L | No limit |
| Capital gains | Not allowed | ✅ Full support |
| Foreign income | Not allowed | ✅ With DTAA credit |
| Multiple properties | Not allowed | ✅ |
| Crypto/VDA | Not allowed | ✅ (30% flat) |
| Loss carry-forward | Not allowed | ✅ (8 years) |

## Capital Gains Set-Off Rules
- STCG loss can offset LTCG (same year)
- LTCG loss can offset STCG (same year)
- Net CG loss carries forward for 8 years
- CG loss CANNOT offset salary/other income

## Surcharge
- Surcharge on LTCG u/s 112A is **capped at 15%** regardless of income level
- Other income: normal surcharge rates (10% > ₹50L, 15% > ₹1Cr, 25% > ₹2Cr)
