# ITR-3 — Step-by-Step Filing Guide

> For individuals with business or professional income (regular books of accounts)

## Who Should File ITR-3?
- Business income with regular books of accounts
- Professional income (doctor, lawyer, CA, consultant) with receipts > ₹75L
- Business turnover > ₹2 crore (or > ₹3Cr if 95%+ digital)
- Freelancers NOT opting for presumptive taxation
- Partners in a firm (share of profit is exempt, but salary/interest from firm is taxable)
- Anyone with business loss to carry forward

## Step-by-Step Flow on BurnBlack

### Step 1–2: Same as ITR-1/2
Start filing, fill personal information.

### Step 3: Business Income
1. Add **"Business"** source in the left panel
2. Click **"Add Business"**
3. Fill business details:
   - Business name, nature of business (code)
   - **Method 1 — From P&L Statement**:
     - Gross turnover/receipts
     - Gross profit
     - Expenses (itemized):
       - Rent, Salaries, Interest, Depreciation
       - Repairs, Insurance, Utilities, Travel
       - Professional fees, Office expenses, Other
     - Net profit = Gross profit - Total expenses
   - **Method 2 — Direct entry**:
     - Net profit (if P&L already prepared)
4. System flags if **audit required**:
   - Business turnover > ₹1 crore → Tax audit u/s 44AB
   - Professional receipts > ₹50 lakh → Tax audit u/s 44AB

### Step 4: Salary Income (if applicable)
Same as ITR-1. Many business owners also draw salary from their company.

### Step 5: Capital Gains (if applicable)
Same as ITR-2. Full capital gains support.

### Step 6: Foreign Income (if applicable)
Same as ITR-2.

### Step 7: Other Income
Same as ITR-1.

### Step 8: Deductions
Same as ITR-1 (80C, 80D, etc. for old regime).
**Note**: Business expenses are already deducted from business income — don't double-claim.

### Step 9: Bank Details & Submit
Same as ITR-1.

## Key Differences from ITR-4 (Presumptive)
| Feature | ITR-3 | ITR-4 |
|---------|-------|-------|
| Books of accounts | Required | Not required |
| Expense deduction | Itemized | Deemed (8%/6%/50%) |
| Turnover limit | No limit | ₹2Cr (business) / ₹75L (profession) |
| Tax audit | If turnover > ₹1Cr | Not required |
| Loss carry-forward | ✅ | ❌ |
| Balance sheet | Required | Not required |

## When to Switch from ITR-4 to ITR-3
- Business turnover exceeds ₹2 crore (or ₹3Cr if 95%+ digital)
- Professional receipts exceed ₹75 lakh
- You want to claim actual expenses (higher than deemed 8%/6%/50%)
- You have business losses to carry forward
- You have both presumptive AND regular business entries

## Tax Computation
Same slab rates as ITR-1/2. Business income is added to gross total income and taxed at slab rates.

### Depreciation
- Depreciation on business assets reduces taxable profit
- Rates: 15% (buildings), 40% (machinery), 60% (computers)
- Additional depreciation: 20% on new plant/machinery in first year

### Audit Requirement
| Condition | Audit Required? |
|-----------|----------------|
| Business turnover > ₹1Cr | Yes (44AB) |
| Professional receipts > ₹50L | Yes (44AB) |
| Turnover ₹1-10Cr + cash < 5% | No (44AB proviso) |
| Opted out of 44AD with turnover < ₹2Cr | Yes for next 5 years |
